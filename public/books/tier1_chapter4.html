Section 1 of 5

🍕The pizza problem.

Hi! 🐵 Today is pizza day. I made 1 pizza. Kiwi 🐨 came over. Now we have 2 friends and 1 pizza.

If we share fairly, how big is each piece? Half a pizza each — because there are 2 of us.

🍕

That's division: "sharing one thing into equal parts." In Python, the divide sign is / (a slash). To split 1 pizza between 2 friends:

share = 1 / 2

Python says 0.5. That's "half." It's the same as the fraction ½. They mean the same thing — just written differently.

/Divide. "Split this into equal parts."

fractionA way to write part of a whole. ½ = 1 piece out of 2.

Section 3 of 5

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦What if more friends come?

Word spreads. Now 4 friends show up. Same one pizza. How big is each piece now?

The fraction is 1 / 4 — one piece out of four. We call it "one-quarter" or "one-fourth." Python writes it 0.25.

Compare

More friends = smaller pieces. Same pizza, divided more ways. 1/2 = 0.5. 1/4 = 0.25. 1/8 = 0.125. The bottom number gets bigger, the piece gets smaller!

You'll get to slice 1 pizza into 4 equal pieces in the practice lab. Next: what if we have more than one pizza? Turn the page. →

Section 4 of 5

🍕🍕🍕What if we have more pizzas?

Now imagine I have 3 pizzas and 4 friends. Sharing is still fair: every friend gets the same. The math is just 3 / 4.

share = 3 / 4 # → 0.75

That's "three-quarters", written ¾, or 0.75 as a decimal. Three out of four pieces — almost a whole pizza each! 🎉

3 / 4Three pieces out of four. Same as ¾ or 0.75.

You'll share 3 pizzas between 4 friends in the practice lab. Last idea before the lab: a way to check whether a share is exactly even. Turn the page. →

Section 5 of 5

⚖️Was the share even?

Sometimes the share comes out to a whole number (like 2 pizzas ÷ 2 friends = 1 each). Sometimes it doesn't (like 1 ÷ 3 = 0.333... it never ends!).

Python has a special check called % (percent sign) — it tells you the leftover after dividing. If the leftover is 0, the share was even.

leftover = 10 % 5 # 0 — even share!
leftover = 10 % 3 # 1 — uneven, 1 left over
%"Modulo" — the leftover after dividing.

You'll use both / and % together in the last lab exercise.

What you learned

  • / means divide — split into equal parts.
  • 1/2 = 0.5 is "half." Same as the fraction ½.
  • More friends = smaller pieces (1/4 = 0.25).
  • You can divide more than one whole: 3/4 = 0.75.
  • % tells you the leftover after dividing.
  • If leftover is 0, the share is fair!
