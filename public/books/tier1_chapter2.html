Section 2 of 5

➕Adding two variables.

Last chapter, we made one box. Now let's make two — one for me, one for Kiwi:

mango = 4
kiwi = 3

Now here's the cool part. We can put a third box in the middle that holds the total of both:

total = mango + kiwi

Read it slowly: "Look in the box mango, look in the box kiwi, add what you find, and put the answer in a new box called total."

+Just like in math — add two numbers.
totalA new box. The name is up to you. We could call it everything or both.

Section 3 of 5

➖Taking some away.

One sunny afternoon, I had 10 bananas. Then a little parrot 🦜 zoomed by and grabbed 4 of them! How many did I have left?

In Python, "take away" looks like this:

bananas = 10
bananas = bananas - 4

That second line says: "Look in the box bananas, take 4 away, put the answer back in the same box." The minus sign − works just like in math class — but the box is doing the work.

Watch out

If you take away more than you have, the answer goes below zero. 5 − 8 = −3. The computer doesn't get embarrassed about negative numbers — it just shows them.

You'll get to take bananas away yourself in the practice lab at the end of the chapter. Next, a shortcut that real coders use every single day. Turn the page. →

Section 4 of 5

⚡A handy shortcut: +=

Coders are a little lazy (in a smart way). They like shortcuts. Look at these two lines — they do exactly the same thing:

bananas = bananas + 5
bananas += 5

The little symbol += is read as "add this to the box." Same idea — less typing. There's a −= too, for subtracting:

bananas -= 2

That means "take 2 away from the box."

+=Add the number on the right to the box.
-=Take the number on the right away from the box.

You'll practice += and -= in the lab at the end. One more thing first: a real story problem that needs all of these tools at once. Turn the page. →

Section 5 of 5

🧩A real story problem.

Here comes the moment when code becomes useful. We'll solve a real little word problem with a few lines of Python.

The story

Mango has 12 bananas. He gives Kiwi 5. Then Kiwi shares 2 back. How many bananas does Mango have now?

The trick: start with 12, take away 5, then add 2 back. The answer should be 9. You've now seen everything you need!

What you learned

  • You can have more than one variable at a time.
  • + adds two numbers (or two variables).
  • − takes some away.
  • += is a shortcut for "add to the box."
  • -= is a shortcut for "take away from the box."
  • A few lines of code can solve a whole word problem.
