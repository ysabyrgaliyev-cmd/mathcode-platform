General Sinusoidal Form: y = A · func(B · (x − C)) + D

A (Amplitude) controls the wave's height — how far it stretches above and below the center line. |A| is the distance from center to peak.
B (Frequency) controls how fast the wave oscillates. Period = 2π/B. Higher B means more cycles in the same space.
C (Phase Shift) slides the wave left or right. Positive C shifts right, negative shifts left.
D (Vertical Shift) moves the entire wave up or down. The new center line is y = D.

Key Identity: cos(x) = sin(x + π/2) — cosine is just a phase-shifted sine wave!
