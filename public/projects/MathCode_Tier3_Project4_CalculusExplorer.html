The Derivative: The slope of the tangent line at a point measures how fast f(x) is changing — this is the instantaneous rate of change.

From Secant to Tangent: The secant line through (x₀, f(x₀)) and (x₀+h, f(x₀+h)) has slope [f(x₀+h) − f(x₀)] / h. As h → 0, this secant approaches the tangent. The limit is f'(x₀).

Try it: Drag the h slider toward 0 and watch the secant line become the tangent line!
