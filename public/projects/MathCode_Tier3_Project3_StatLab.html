Central Tendency: Mean (average of all values), Median (middle value when sorted), Mode (most frequent value).

Spread: Range = max − min. Standard Deviation measures how far values typically are from the mean. A small σ means data is clustered; a large σ means it's spread out.

Quartiles & IQR: Q1 (25th percentile), Q2 (median), Q3 (75th percentile). IQR = Q3 − Q1 contains the middle 50% of data. Outliers lie beyond Q1 − 1.5·IQR or Q3 + 1.5·IQR.
